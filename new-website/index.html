Neural Cellular Automata

In this project, I developed and trained various Neural cellular automata (NCA) models. A cellular automaton is a simulation which consist of a collection many cells which work together to self-organize in order to achieve a certain goal - the process alltogether is an example of emergence. NCA models make use of a neural-network and are capable of more complex behaviors such as being able to regenerate after receiving cellular damage. To learn more about NCA, visit the project's website and or check out my Master's thesis.


Continuous Cellular Automata

As a precursor to my NCA work, this project showcases the beautiful emergent complexity found in cellular automata. Traditonal cellular automata (such as Conway's Game of Life) restrict the cells to a finite number of discrete states (e.g. 0 or 1). Continuous cellular automata (CCA) allow a cell's state to be any continuous number between 0 and 1. When paired with an activation function, the resulting animations disply intricate and life-like interactions, emblematic of emergence in mathematics and nature. Check out the project's website for an interactive demo. Images taken from this project also won a visualization contest hosted by the College of Natural Sciences at The University of Texas during my time as a graduate student.
